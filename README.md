> An end-to-end data analysis project using **MySQL** and **Power BI** to analyze customer churn, retention, and revenue performance for a telecom company serving 200 customers across 5 East African countries.

## 💡 Key Insights

- Churn rate is **30.50%**, above the 25% business target — immediate action needed
- **Standard Router** plan has the highest churn at **39%**
- **Uganda** leads churn by country at **37%**
- Churn peaks in **January and June** — a seasonal pattern worth investigating
- **Business Fiber** generates the highest retained revenue at **$72.08K**

## 🗂️ Dataset

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| `customer_id` | Unique customer identifier |
| `customer_name` | Full name |
| `country` | Somalia, Ethiopia, Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania |
| `plan_type` | Basic Mobile, Standard Router, Premium Bundle, Business Fiber |
| `monthly_charge` | Monthly subscription fee |
| `subscription_start` | Date customer joined |
| `subscription_end` | Date of cancellation — NULL if still active |
| `is_churned` | 1 = Churned · 0 = Active |
| `tenure_months` | Months subscribed |
| `avg_monthly_gb` | Average monthly data usage |
| `support_tickets` | Number of complaints raised |
| `total_revenue` | Total revenue generated by the customer |

**Churn definition:** A customer is churned when their subscription is explicitly cancelled (`is_churned = 1`, `subscription_end IS NOT NULL`). Active customers have no end date — by design, not by error. This ensures churn rate and retention rate always sum to exactly 100%.

## 🔍 SQL Analysis — 5 Sections

**Section 1 — Database & Table Setup**
Created the database schema with correct data types.

**Section 2 — Data Validation**
Checked for missing values, verified churn flag consistency, and confirmed no duplicates.

**Section 3 — Customer & Churn Overview**
Calculated total customers, churn rate, retention rate, and churn breakdown by plan type and country.

**Section 4 — Revenue Analysis**
Computed total revenue, active revenue, revenue lost to churn, and average customer value — broken down by plan type with top 10 customers ranked.

**Section 5 — Monthly Subscription Trend**
Tracked new subscriptions and cancellations month by month to identify seasonal churn patterns.

---

## 💼 Business Recommendations

1. **Investigate Standard Router plan** — 39% churn is the highest across all plans. Pricing or service quality may be driving cancellations.
2. **Focus retention on Uganda** — highest churn country at 37%. A targeted campaign could recover meaningful revenue.
3. **Address January and June spikes** — proactive outreach before these months could reduce seasonal losses.
4. **Protect Business Fiber customers** — highest retained revenue segment. Priority support and loyalty incentives are justified.
